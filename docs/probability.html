3 Probability

The probability of event \(E\), \(\mathbb{P} (E)\), is defined as \[ \lim{N \to \infty} \frac{\text{number of occurrences of }E}{N}, \] where \(N\) is the number of independent trials.

3.1 5 rules of probability

  1. \(0 \leq \mathbb{P}(E) \leq 1.\)
  2. \(\mathbb{P} (\emptyset) = 0.\)
  3. \(\mathbb{P} (\Omega) = 1\), where \(\Omega\) is the whole sample space.
  4. If \(E\) is a disjoint union of events \(E_1, \ldots, E_k\), then \[ \mathbb{P}E = \sum_{i = 1}^k \mathbb{P}E_i. \]

3.2 Independence

If \(\mathbb{P}(A \cap B) = \mathbb{P}(A) \mathbb{P}(B)\) or \(\mathbb{P}(A\vert B) = \mathbb{P}(A)\), then \(A\) and \(B\) are independent.
